Section D

Real-World Applications

NLP applications where Week 1 concepts are foundational, with explanations of relevant techniques, necessity, and expected benefits.

Search Engines
Relevant Concepts

Tokenization, Stop-word Removal, TF-IDF, Word Embeddings, BERT

Why Needed

Must understand queries and match to relevant documents. Raw text matching fails due to synonymy and polysemy.

Expected Benefits

TF-IDF ensures distinctive query terms rank higher; embeddings enable semantic search beyond keywords; BERT understands query context (e.g., "jaguar" as animal vs. car).

Chatbots & Conversational AI
Relevant Concepts

Lemmatization, Word Embeddings, Transformers, Contextual Embeddings, Intent Classification

Why Needed

Must parse user intent, maintain conversation context, and generate coherent responses across multi-turn dialogues.

Expected Benefits

Embeddings map similar intents together; Transformers maintain context across turns; GPT-4 generates human-like responses at scale.

Sentiment Analysis
Relevant Concepts

Text Cleaning, Lemmatization, Contextual Embeddings, TF-IDF

Why Needed

Requires clean text to identify opinion-bearing words. Lemmatization preserves negation. Contextual embeddings distinguish slang from literal meaning.

Expected Benefits

Automated analysis of millions of reviews; real-time brand monitoring; feature-specific sentiment extraction (e.g., "battery life" → positive).

Machine Translation
Relevant Concepts

Subword Tokenization (BPE/SentencePiece), Neural LMs, Attention, Contextual Embeddings

Why Needed

Requires understanding source semantics and generating target text. Subword tokenization handles rare words across languages.

Expected Benefits

Near-human quality for many language pairs; real-time translation between 100+ languages; proper handling of technical terms and names.

Text Summarization
Relevant Concepts

Sentence Segmentation, TF-IDF, Extractive vs. Abstractive, BART/T5/PEGASUS

Why Needed

Must identify salient information and condense it. TF-IDF scores sentence importance; abstractive models generate novel summary sentences.

Expected Benefits

Condense 10-page documents to 1-paragraph summaries; process thousands of articles automatically; generate fluent, connected summaries via T5.

Clinical NLP
Relevant Concepts

Text Cleaning (medical abbreviations), Lemmatization, NER, BioBERT/ClinicalBERT

Why Needed

Medical records contain noisy, abbreviated text with life-critical information. Precision is paramount.

Expected Benefits

Accurate extraction of diagnoses and medications; automated ICD-10 coding; large-scale EHR analysis for drug discovery; real-time drug interaction alerts.
